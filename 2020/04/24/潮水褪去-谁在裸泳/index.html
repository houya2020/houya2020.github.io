潮水褪去 谁在裸泳

Posted on 2020-04-24

Corporate fraud

公司欺诈

Who‘s lost their trunks?

谁在裸泳

The crisis will expose a decade’s worth of swindling and aggressive accounting

这场危机会让十年来的欺诈和基金会计法暴露无遗

When BERNIE MADOFF owned up to a $65bn Ponzi scheme in December 2008 ,it was not out of guilt.He knew the game was up.There months earlier Lehman Brothers had imploded. The market meltdown sent clients clamouring to withdraw from his funds,leaving them depleted with many investors still unpaid. American regulators had not spotted the fraud,despite a tip-off years earlier. It was not them that did for Mr Madoff,but recession.

2008年12月，伯尼·麦道夫承认策划了一场规模高达650亿美元的庞氏骗局。但他并非于心有愧，而是意识到游戏已经结束。同年9月，莱曼兄弟倒台。市场崩盘，客户纷纷要求撤出他的基金，导致基金所剩无几，许多投资者至今仍未得到偿付。尽管早在几年前就有人举报，但美国监管机构仍未发现欺诈行为。揭发麦道夫骗局的不是美国监管机构，而是经济的衰退。

Booms help fraudsters paper over cracks in their accounts,from fictitious investment returns to exaggerated sales.Slowdowns rip the covering off.As Baruch Lev,an accounting professor at New York University,puts it,”In good times everyone looks good, and the market punishes you harshly for not keeping up.” Many big book-cooking scandals of the past 20 years emerged in downturns. A decade before the crisis of 2007-09 the dotcom crash exposed accounting sins at Enron and WorldCom perpetrated in the go-go late 1990s.Both firms went bust soon after.As Warren Buffet，a revered investor, once put it:”You only find out who is swimming anked when the tide goes out.” This time,thanks to a pandemic, the water has whooshed away at record speed.

经济繁荣时期，企业可以捏造投资回报，夸大销售额，在账目上瞒天过海。经济一旦放缓，这层遮羞布便会被撕掉。正如纽约大学会计学教授巴鲁克·列夫所言：“繁荣的时代，所有人看上去都不错，然而一旦跟不上节奏，市场就会严厉惩罚你。”过去二十年里很多重大财务造假丑闻都是在经济衰退时浮出水面。在2007-2009年金融危机爆发前十年，互联网泡沫破裂让上世纪90年代末繁荣时期安然和世通财务造假罪行昭然若揭。不久两家公司便以破产而告终。正如万人敬仰的Warren Buffet所言：“只有当潮水褪去，你才知道谁一直在裸泳。”此次新冠疫情肆虐，退潮速度之快史无前例。

Hell and low water

潮水退去，如在炼狱

Much of the swimwear was already threadbare: a borrowing binge has strained many corporate balance-sheets.Some dirty secrets are beginning to come out.Take Luckin Coffee,which had expanded to take on Starbucks in China,attracting big-name investors like Blackrock and Singapore’s sovereign-wealth fund.On April 2nd the Nasdaq-listed Chinese chain announced an ongoing internal probe amid allegations that its chief operating officer and other employees may have fabricated over 2bn yuan($280m) in sales.On April 14 th Citron Research,a short_seller ,accused GSX, a Chinese online-tutoring firm listed in New York,of inflating last year’s sales.In a statment GSX denied the allegations and said Citron’s report was misleading and “full of subjective maliciousness “.

很多泳装已破旧不堪，许多公司因疯狂借贷，资产负债表早已不堪重负。一些肮脏的秘密开始浮现。以瑞星咖啡为例，瑞幸曾大举扩张，意欲在中国与星巴克一较高下，吸引了Blackrock和新加坡主权财富基金等大牌投资者。而在4月2日，这家在纳斯达克上市的中国连锁咖啡企业宣布正在进行一项内部调查，回应有关其首席运营官和其他员工涉及伪造20多亿元销售额的言论。4月14日，做空机构Citron Research称在纽约上市的中国在线教育公司跟谁学夸大了去年的销售额。跟谁学在一份声明中否认了这些言论，并表示该报告企图误导投资者和公众，充满了主观恶意。

These revelations have revived fears over the flaky corporate governance of Chinese firms listed on foreign exchanges,whose audits,conducted at home,China’s government makes it hard for outsiders to inspect. A gaggle of fraud-hunters like Citron and Muddy Waters,which outed Luckin,claimed numerous scalps after the first wave of such listings a decade ago.This time they are looking beyond China.

这些披露再次引发了人们对境外上市的中国企业管理不力的担忧。这些公司的审计工作在国内进行，ZG政府的监管使外界很难对其进行调查。自十年前第一波中国企业上市潮后，Citron和Muddy Waters等一群做空机构蜂拥而上，俘获的战利品数不胜数。这一次，他们将目光投向了中国之外。

Blue Orca Capital,an Asia-focused fund targeting corporate “zeros”,expects opportunities to pop up in other emerging markets,Europe and America.”My entire career has been in a bull market,”says its founder,Soren Aandahl.”This is exciting.”Mr Aandahl is eyeing any firms with discrepancies between the amount of capital they need to raise and the cash their accounts say they are generating. Others are focusing on industries hit hardest by the pandemic,such as travel,entertainment and properity.

杀人鲸资本是一家专注于亚洲的基金，目标是企业零息债券，它预计欧美及其他新兴市场会涌现很多机会。“我的整个职业生涯都在牛市中，”其创始人索仁·阿安德尔说，“真让人兴奋。”阿安德尔关注的是账目显示收入现金与需筹集资本额不匹配的公司。其他做空机构则将重点放在疫情影响最严重的行业，例如旅游、娱乐、房地产。

Only a small minority of firms resort to outright fraud. Far more prettify profit-and-loss statements with accounting wheezes that fall in a grey area.This accounts for much of what John Kenneth Galbraith,an economist,called “the bezzle” and “psychic wealth”: gains that appear real but prove illusory.

只有小部分公司敢明目张胆造假。大部分是耍一些灰色地带的会计花招美化损益表。这就是经济学家约翰·肯尼思·加尔布雷思所谓的“未发现侵吞公款额”和“精神上的国民财富”，看似真实，实则虚幻的收益。

In the bull market startups became masters of conjuring up novel metrics that flatter performance. WeWork’s “community-adjusted”earning before interest ,taxes,depreciation and amortisation transformed a hefty loss for 2018 under Generally Accepted Accounting Principles into a profit. Illegal ? No.A red flag?Absolutely .Many investors turned a blind eye because they bought into what Mr Aandahl calls” the myth in the shareholder list”:all would be well if other high-profile backers were on board(as with Luckin)

在牛市中，初创公司特别善于制定新颖的指标，花式鼓吹自家业绩。2018年Wework所谓“社区调整”后的息税折旧摊销前利润看似盈利，若按照公认会计标准来衡量，则表现为巨额亏损。违法吗？不，但足以给我们警示。许多投资者视而不见，因为他们信了阿安德尔先生所谓的“股东的迷信”：只要其他大金主还在，一切就都没问题（瑞幸便是一例）。

Non-GAAP adjustments have spread like wildfire through corporate accounts,making it harder to discern what numbers reflect a firm’s true financial position.The average number of num-GAAP measures used in filings by companies in the S&P 500 index has increased from 2.5 to 7.5 in the past 20 years,according to PWC a consultancy.In credit agreements analysised bu Zion Research Group,the definition of EBITDA ranges from 75 words to over 2,200.GAAP is far from perfect,but some of the divergence from it has clearly been designed to pull wool over investors’ eyes.One study found that non-GAAP profits were,on average,15% higher than GAAP profits.

各公司纷纷对帐目进行非公认会计准则调整，势如燎原之火。在此之下，哪些数字才能反映一个公司的真实财务状况变得越发模糊了。据咨询公司普华永道统计，过去20年里标普500指数公司所提交的文件中，所使用的非公认会计准则指标的平均数量已经从2.5个上升到7.5个。锡安研究机构所分析的信贷协议中，息税折旧摊销前利润的定义用词从75个增加到2200个。公认会计准则远非完美，但显然，有些与之相异的规则就是专门用来蒙蔽投资者的。一项研究发现，非公认会计准则之下的公司利润比公认会计准则平均高出15%。

Playing around with earnings and revenue-recognition metrics is this generation’s equivalent of dotcoms using bots and other tricks to boost “eyeballs” 20 years ago,says Jules Kroll of k2 Intelligence, the doyen of corporate sleuths.”When an area is hot to the point of overheated,there is a growing temptation to juice the numbers.” In an ominous sign,SoftBank ,a Japanese technology conglomerate which bet big on WeWork and dozens of other startups,said this week that it expects an operating loss of ￥1.4trn($12.5bn) in its last fiscal year.

这一代人玩收益收入的核算指标，跟20年前互联网公司利用机器人等伎俩来吸引“眼球”没什么区别，资深企业调查机构K2 Intelligence的Jules Kroll说道，“哪个领域发展的越快越热，就越可能给数字掺水分.”日本科技集团软银公司（在WeWork和其他许多初创公司持有巨额股份）本周表示，预计上一财年运营亏损达1.4万亿日元（约合790亿元人民币），实属不祥之兆。

Besides exposing old schemes, the pandemic is likely to give rise to new ones.When economic survival is threatened ,the line separating what is acceptable and unacceptable when booking revenues or making market disclosures can be blurred.Mr Kroll reckons that “amid such massive dislocation, some will inevitably cheat.”

疫情不仅揭露了旧骗局，还可能引发新骗局。一旦危机经济存亡，记录账面收入或披露市场信息时，能与不能之间的界限就会模糊。据克罗尔推测，市场如此动荡，企业造假无法避免。

Bruce Dorris，head of the Association of Certified Fraud Examiners,the world’s largest anti-fraud outfit,says the effects of covid-19 look like “a perfect storm for fraud.” It may engender everthing from iffy accounting to stimulus-linked scams as thousands of firms-including bogus applicants-hustle for help.One fraud investigator points to private-equity-owned firms as potential targets.”There are lots of them,they are highly leveraged and they may not qualify for bail-outs because thay have deep-pocketed ponsors,” he says.That increases the temptation to resort to unseemly practices.The ebbing tide is likely to reveal plenty of corporate nudity.That will not stop some business from taking up naturism.

美国注册舞弊审查师协会（ACFE）是全球最大的反欺诈机构，其负责人布鲁斯·多里斯称，新冠肺炎的影响看起来像一场“欺诈的完美风暴”。它可能会诱发做假账及骗取刺激的各种问题，因为成千上万（不乏伪造申请）的公司都在慌忙寻求救助。一位反欺诈调查人员指出，私募股权所有制公司就是潜在的调查对象。他表示，“它们数量众多，杠杆率很高，可能不符合救助资格，因为其赞助方实力雄厚。”这样一来，企业愈发会采取见不得人的操作。潮水退去，会让很多裸泳的公司原形毕露。江山易改，但有些公司本性难移。
